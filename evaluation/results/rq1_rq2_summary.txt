--- Interpretation ---
Raw unsupported-claim rates are similar between the two systems (InvestIQ 0.533 vs Baseline 0.552 over 59 annotated claims), so the multi-agent structure does not by itself reduce ungrounded claims. Where they diverge is auditability: InvestIQ's structural citation field always exposes the full retrieved evidence set (coverage 1.000), honestly surfacing the evidence actually used even when it is weak — as in TSLA, where coverage stays 1.000 over off-topic evidence — whereas the baseline only exposes the inline citations it chose to embed (coverage 0.520). When the baseline does cite it is mostly faithful (12/14 = 0.857), but the 2 unfaithful cases are silent overreach — a real E-id attached to a claim the cited evidence does not support.
