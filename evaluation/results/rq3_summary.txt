--- Interpretation ---
FinBERT substantially outperforms the lexicon baseline on domain-specific financial sentiment: FinBERT reaches 91.5% accuracy and 0.894 macro-F1, versus VADER's 57.1% and 0.487 — an absolute accuracy gap of 34.4%. The divergence is largest on the negative class (per-class F1 0.888 for FinBERT vs 0.279 for VADER). This reflects a fundamental limitation of general-purpose lexicons in finance: VADER keys on surface tokens such as 'profit', 'loss', 'growth', or 'decline' and so misclassifies factual, neutral financial statements as strongly positive or negative, while FinBERT — fine-tuned on financial tone — captures the reporting context and correctly assigns neutrality. For InvestIQ this confirms that a domain-adapted transformer is necessary for trustworthy sentiment signals, and justifies FinBERT's additional inference cost over a lightweight rule-based baseline.
